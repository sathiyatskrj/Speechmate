The Nicobar Islands are a rugged, forested archipelago in the eastern Indian Ocean, lying about 1,300 km southeast of the Indian mainland. They serve as a boundary between the Bay of Bengal and the Andaman Sea.
- **Islands**: 22 islands (12 inhabited).
- **Major Islands**: Car Nicobar (North), Nancowry & Kamorta (Central), Great Nicobar (South).

- **Climate**: Tropical, hot, humid, with heavy monsoon rains.
